Recognizing Microaggressions
Towards Indigenous Peoples

Welcome, learner!

Microaggressions are the everyday verbal, nonverbal, and environmental slights, snubs, or insults, whether intentional or unintentional, that communicate hostile, derogatory, or negative messages to target persons based solely upon their marginalized group membership. (Sue et al., 2017)
  • Verbal: e.g., “Where are you really from?”
  • Non-verbal: e.g., clutching a bag when someone approaches.
  • Environmental: e.g., a lack of diverse representation in leadership roles.

1. Model Curiosity

Ask Questions

Encourage reflection by asking open-ended questions to understand the comment better and promote discussion.

“Can you tell me more about that?”
“Help us understand what you mean?”
“What do you think informs that perspective?”

This is a flip card. Activated by pressing enter or spacebar.
2. Acknowledge the Incident

Pause and Address

Stop the conversation to directly address the issue and create a safe space for learning.

“I want to pause and address something that was just said. It’s important that we create a respectful space where harmful assumptions or stereotypes are avoided.”

This is a flip card. Activated by pressing enter or spacebar.
3. Avoid Shaming

Stay Constructive

Focus on the comment or behavior, not the person, to avoid defensiveness. Keep the tone educational, not accusatory.

“Let’s talk about the comment and why it might not be helpful, rather than focusing on who said it.”

This is a flip card. Activated by pressing enter or spacebar.
4. Reframe the Statement

Clarify Intentions

Reword the comment to help the group engage with it in a way that promotes understanding and minimizes bias.

“Perhaps what you meant to express was..."
Let’s explore that from a different angle to ensure everyone feels heard and respected.”

This is a flip card. Activated by pressing enter or spacebar.
5. Provide Context

Explain the Impact

Briefly explain how the comment may unintentionally perpetuate biases and why that’s problematic.

“That comment might unintentionally reinforce stereotypes. It’s important to recognize that language shapes our understanding of different groups.”
